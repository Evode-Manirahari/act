# ACT Capture — 60-Day Pilot

**Capture how your best techs diagnose your hardest jobs — before they retire — and turn it into company-specific training that cuts callbacks and gets new hires billable on your own install base.**

## The problem you already track

- **Callbacks cost ~$650 each** (ACCA) — and the techs who don't generate them are closest to retirement.
- **New hires take months to get billable** on *your* accounts and equipment mix — which generic training can't teach.
- **When a 30-year tech retires, the judgment leaves with them.** Nobody writes it down.

## What ACT Capture does

Your senior tech records the job from their phone and taps **Mark** at the moments that matter — one-handed, glove-friendly, nothing to write. Afterward, ACT asks them three short questions per moment; the answers become training cards: **clip + the expert's why + the novice trap + the safety boundary + a quiz.** ACT sits **on top of** generic sim training like Interplay — that teaches the textbook; ACT captures what only your company knows.

## The 60-day pilot

| | |
|---|---|
| **Scope** | One region, one senior/retiring tech, your callback-prone accounts |
| **Capture** | ~20 real jobs over 6 weeks, ≤5 min of senior-tech time per job |
| **Output** | 50 reviewed, company-specific cards — published weekly, not at the end |
| **Measurement** | Weeks 7–10: apprentice scores pre/post, callback + first-time-fix delta vs. prior 90 days |

## Cost and guarantee

**Flat $5,000** for the full 60 days, all-inclusive. **Fewer than 30 published cards: full refund.** No commitment; cancel anytime. Post-pilot rollout, if the numbers earn it, is per-tech per-month across the region — a rounding error against one avoided callback per tech per quarter.

## What we need — and what your tech controls

One senior tech (10+ yrs) willing to be recorded, two or more newer techs as the learning side, and your prior-90-day callback numbers as the baseline. The senior tech owns the marks and approves every clip, question, and card — nothing publishes without them. No performance scoring; customers get a printed notice and can opt out on any visit. Cards credit the expert by name.
